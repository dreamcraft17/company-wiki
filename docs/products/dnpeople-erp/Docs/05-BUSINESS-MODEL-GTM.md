## 1. BUSINESS MODEL

### 1.1 SaaS Subscription Model

#### Pricing Tiers
```
Tier 1: STARTUP
Price: $299/month
Users: Up to 5
Features:
- Core modules (GL, AP, AR, Sales, Basic Inventory)
- Up to 10,000 transactions/month
- 10GB storage
- Basic support (Email, 24hr response)
- Community forum access
- Single company

Tier 2: PROFESSIONAL
Price: $999/month
Users: Up to 25
Features:
- All STARTUP features
- + Manufacturing module
- + Advanced CRM
- + Projects management
- + Up to 100,000 transactions/month
- 100GB storage
- Priority support (Email/Chat, 4hr response)
- API access (1,000 calls/day)
- Multi-company support (3)
- Custom reports builder
- Integrations (5 third-party apps)

Tier 3: ENTERPRISE
Price: Custom (typically $3,000+/month)
Users: Unlimited
Features:
- All PROFESSIONAL features
- + Advanced analytics & BI
- + Custom workflows
- + Advanced security features
- + Unlimited transactions
- + Unlimited storage
- + 24/7 phone & dedicated support
- + Unlimited API calls
- + Unlimited companies
- + Dedicated account manager
- + Custom integrations
- + SLA guarantee (99.9%)
- + Training & onboarding

Add-ons:
- Extra users: $50/user/month
- Extra storage (100GB): $50/month
- Advanced analytics: $500/month
- Custom development: $150/hour
- Training & onboarding: $5,000/package
```

#### Revenue Model
```
Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR):
= (Startup × 299) + (Professional × 999) + (Enterprise ARR / 12)

Assumptions Year 1:
- Month 1-2: 5 Startup, 2 Professional = $1,448/month
- Month 3-4: 10 Startup, 5 Professional = $6,445/month
- Month 5-6: 20 Startup, 10 Professional, 1 Enterprise = $16,180/month
- Month 7-12: Growth rate 15-20% month-over-month

Year 1 Projections:
- Startup tier: 30-40 customers
- Professional tier: 10-15 customers
- Enterprise tier: 1-2 customers
- Estimated Year 1 ARR: $500K - $800K
- Year 1 MRR by end: $50K-65K

Year 2 Projections:
- Customer base growth: 3x (100+ customers)
- Estimated Year 2 ARR: $1.5M - $2M

Year 3+ Projections:
- Market leader position
- 300+ customers
- $3M+ ARR
```

├─ Engineering (4 FTE): $600

   Subtotal: $1,250 (125%)

Other Costs:
├─ Tools & licenses (GitHub, monitoring, etc): $50
├─ Payment processing (2.2% of transactions): $25
├─ Customer acquisition (CAC buffer): $100
└─ Miscellaneous: $25
   Subtotal: $200 (20%)

Total Cost: $1,730 per $1,000 MRR
(Cost increase due to team scaling, reduces as MRR grows)

Year 1 Burn Rate:
- Average MRR: $25,000

```

### 2.2 Unit Economics

```
Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):
- Average sales cycle: 30 days
- Inbound leads cost: $1,000/customer
- Direct sales cost: $3,000/customer
- Blended CAC: $2,000

Customer Lifetime Value (LTV):
- Average subscription length: 3 years
- Average monthly value (Startup tier): $299
- Churn rate: 5%/month (industry average)
- Professional tier mix: 20% higher average value
- Average LTV: $8,500

CAC Payback Period:
- LTV / CAC = 4.25x (healthy >3x)
- Months to payback: ~7 months

Subscription Metrics:
- Monthly Churn Rate: 5%
- Annual Churn Rate: ~60% (industry: 40-60% typical)
- Target: Reduce to 3-4% through improvements
- NRR (Net Revenue Retention): 110%
  - Retention: 95%
  - Expansion revenue: 15% additional
```

### 3.2 Sales Strategy

#### Sales Organization

```
Phase 1 (Months 1-4): Founder-led sales
- CEO handles all sales
- Focus: Inbound + warm introductions
- Target: 5 customers

Phase 2 (Months 5-8): Add sales development
- Hire Sales Development Representative (SDR)
- Focus: Outbound prospecting
- Target: 10 additional customers
- Lead generation: LinkedIn, industry forums, events

Phase 3 (Months 9-12): Scale sales team
- Hire Account Executive (AE)
- Hire Customer Success Manager (CSM)
- Focus: Professional + Enterprise
- Target: 15-20 additional customers

Year 2+: Build channel partnerships
- Resellers & integrators
- Implementation partners
- Consulting firms
```

#### Sales Process & Funnel

```
Sales Funnel Assumptions:

Awareness Stage (Inbound):
- Blog content: ERP guides, tips (target keywords: "Indonesia ERP", "SME accounting")
- LinkedIn: 10 posts/week about features, case studies
- Industry events: 2 events/quarter
- Free trial signups: 30-50/month

Interest Stage:
- Free trial conversion: 10% → 3-5 conversion meetings
- Inbound leads conversion: 40% → respond within 4 hours
- Average deal size: $300-1,000/month (mix of tiers)

Decision Stage:
- Sales cycle: 21-30 days (shorter than traditional ERP)
- Close rate: 30-40% of qualified leads
- Decision makers: Finance manager + operations

Close & Retain:
- Implementation time: 2-4 weeks
- Time to value: 4-8 weeks
- Onboarding: Guided setup + training
- NPS target: 40+ in first 3 months

Sales Metrics:
- Monthly sales activities: 100+ per AE
- Lead response time: <4 hours
- Sales cycle: 30 days
- Win rate: 25-30%
- Average deal value growth: 20%/quarter
```

## 5. COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

### 5.1 Competitive Landscape

```
Tier 1: Enterprise Solutions
- SAP, Oracle, Microsoft Dynamics
- Positioning: Large enterprises
- Price: $100K-$1M+ annually
- Implementation: 6-18 months
- Advantage: Comprehensive, industry-specific
- Disadvantage: Expensive, complex, slow to deploy

Tier 2: Mid-market Solutions
- Odoo, NetSuite, Intacct
- Positioning: Mid-market companies
- Price: $10K-$100K annually
- Implementation: 2-6 months
- Advantage: Balance of features & cost
- Disadvantage: Still complex, moderate learning curve

Tier 3: SME Solutions (OUR SEGMENT)
- Small SME ERP solutions, local accounting software
- Positioning: Small businesses
- Price: $100-$1,000/month
- Implementation: 1-4 weeks
- Advantage: Simple, affordable, local
- Disadvantage: Limited features, less scalable

dnPeople Positioning:
- "Enterprise-grade ERP for SMEs at affordable price"
- Key differentiators:
  1. Ease of use (vs complex solutions)
  2. Fast implementation (vs 6-month projects)
  3. Affordable pricing (vs $100K+ setup)
  4. Local language & support
  5. Industry-specific workflows (manufacturing, distribution)
  6. Modern UX (vs legacy systems)
  7. Cloud-native (no servers to maintain)
  8. Scalable (grow with business)
```

### 5.2 Competitive Advantages

```
Vs Odoo:
- Simpler UI/UX
- Better out-of-box experience
- Dedicated Indonesian market
- Faster implementation
- Better customer support in Indonesian
- Industry-specific templates

Vs Legacy Indonesian ERP:

Vs Accounting Software Only (QuickBooks, Jurnal):

## 9. RISK ASSESSMENT & MITIGATION

### 9.1 Key Risks

```
Technical Risks:
Risk: System scalability issues
Impact: Customer dissatisfaction, churn
Mitigation:
- Load testing throughout development
- Cloud-native architecture from day 1
- Auto-scaling infrastructure
- Performance monitoring

Risk: Data security breaches
Impact: Legal liability, customer trust
Mitigation:
- Security by design
- Regular penetration testing
- Compliance certifications (SOC2, ISO27001)
- Insurance coverage

Market Risks:
Risk: Competition from larger players
Impact: Slower adoption, customer loss
Mitigation:
- Focus on specific segments (SMEs, industries)
- Superior UX/customer experience
- Local market advantages
- Faster innovation cycles

Risk: Low product-market fit
Impact: Slow customer acquisition
Mitigation:
- Continuous customer feedback
- Regular user testing
- Willingness to pivot features/positioning
- Early customer interviews

Financial Risks:
Risk: Funding runway depletion
Impact: Shutdown of operations
Mitigation:
- Conservative burn rate management
- Multiple funding sources explored
- Path to profitability early
- Potential revenue deals (partnership)

Operational Risks:
Risk: Inability to scale operations
Impact: Poor customer service, team burnout
Mitigation:
- Hire experienced operational leaders
- Document processes early
- Invest in operations infrastructure
- Outsource non-core functions
```
